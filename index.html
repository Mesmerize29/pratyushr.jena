The next frontier of intelligent systems

AI will keep getting cheaper and stronger. The real question is what happens when we drop these systems into real environments with people, noise, and moving parts.

I work at the intersection of AI security, robotics, and cyber physical systems. I care about how systems behave when the inputs are weird, the sensors lie, or a human does something no one predicted.

My projects range from LLM security pipelines to drones that hunt for blue tape on the floor. Different tools, same question: does it still work when the world misbehaves?

Patterns I chase

  • How a simple rule breaks inside a complex system.
  • Where vulnerability hides in an interaction, not a line of code.
  • How to borrow ideas from nature to keep systems stable.

Featured work

A few systems that taught me something real about autonomy, safety, and failure.

Micro UAV · Search pattern

Crazyflie search and avoid

Crazyflie spiral search and obstacle avoidance

A tiny drone that performs a spiral search to find a blue tape trail, locks on, and follows it while avoiding walls using optical flow and multiranger decks.

This forced me to treat weight distribution, sensor noise, and controller tuning as one ecosystem instead of separate problems.

  • Spiral search pattern tuned for small indoor spaces.
  • Line detection over noisy optical flow readings.
  • Emergency behaviors when the environment changes suddenly.
Hexacopter · LiDAR

Hexacopter + UGV mode

Hexacopter with LiDAR and hybrid UGV behavior

A custom hexacopter using Navio2, Garmin LiDAR Lite, TF Mini sensors, and Mission Planner. The system blends aerial navigation with ground like behavior.

I focused on autonomy logic and safety checks so that when one thing fails, the whole platform does not silently drift into a bad state.

  • Obstacle sensing and mapping with dual LiDAR.
  • Mode switching between hover, move, and cautious crawl.
  • Telemetry driven debugging loops to catch subtle issues.
Vision · Robotics handoff

Vial cap vision system

Vision guided vial cap detection and mapping

A RealSense based system that detects vial cap colors, segments them into holders A–D, and prepares a clean map for a robotic arm.

Black caps, missing caps, and empty spots are treated consistently so that the robot logic stays simple and robust.

  • Color based segmentation under changing lighting.
  • Stable holder and position indexing for downstream control.
  • Design for imperfect setups that labs actually have.
LLM security · Taxonomy

Interaction centric LLM security

Interaction driven cybersecurity for LLM powered systems

An ongoing research effort that looks at LLM security as a chain of interactions: inputs, internal processing, and outputs.

Instead of collecting tricks, I am building a taxonomy of vulnerabilities and a layered defense pipeline that can plug into real products.

  • Input filters, retrieval guards, and backdoor checks.
  • Output auditing and risk scoring for responses.
  • Structured as an IEEE style review and future toolkit blueprint.
