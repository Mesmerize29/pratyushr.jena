From zero to systems that think

I didn’t enter computer science with a fixed roadmap. Most of what I know started from exactly zero: no plan, no playbook, just problems that refused to wait.

Drones that needed to fly, sensors that wouldn’t talk, models that broke the moment you trusted them. Someone had to figure it out. So I did. Over time, that became the pattern: I learn fast, adapt faster, and build systems that don’t fall apart when reality hits.

Now my focus is simple: AI, cybersecurity, and robotics—making intelligent systems that are not just powerful, but trustworthy.

AI Safety • Security • Research

Research direction

My core research interest sits at the intersection of AI safety, cybersecurity, and human–machine interaction. I care about what happens when real users, real incentives, and real attackers collide with large language models and autonomous systems.

I’m currently developing a systematic view of interaction-centric risks in LLM-powered dialogue systems and exploring layered defense strategies that align technical safeguards with human behavior.

Areas I’m exploring

  • Adversarial dialogue and jailbreaks
  • Trust, misuse, and over-reliance
  • Metrics for defense effectiveness
  • Secure-by-design LLM interfaces

Next steps

I’m preparing for a PhD in Computer Science focused on AI safety and security, and I’m actively looking for collaborators, mentors, and research groups working on these problems.
