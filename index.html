Crazyflie flow and multiranger pipelines

What the system does

A micro drone that uses optical flow and multiranger inputs to explore an area, avoid obstacles, and execute spiral search and line following behavior without GPS.

Why it matters

Edge constrained environments, indoor labs, and tight spaces still need reliable autonomy. This system proves that small hardware can still reason about space and movement.

What I contributed

Integrated flow and distance sensors, experimented with spiral search logic, tuned thresholds for stable behavior, and debugged unstable hardware into predictable flight.

Autonomous hexacopter architecture

What the system does

A coaxial hexacopter with Navio2 and Raspberry Pi at its core, lidar guided navigation, and mission planner controlled flights for semi autonomous operations.

Why it matters

Reliable aerial platforms open doors for inspection, mapping, and research missions that cannot rely on manual piloting.

What I contributed

Helped design and integrate the stack from frame to software, wired and configured lidar, tuned control loops, and worked through telemetry and GPS issues until missions were repeatable.

LiDAR based UGV mode

What the system does

Reuses aerial thinking for ground movement, running lidar powered obstacle awareness on a UGV configuration that shares logic with the hexacopter stack.

Why it matters

A shared planning mindset across air and ground reduces complexity and increases reuse for real deployments.

What I contributed

Extended the lidar and mission logic from flight to ground, reasoned about safe distances, and aligned control behavior with the constraints of the ground platform.

Optical flow reasoning

What the system does

Uses pixel motion fields to estimate direction and relative movement for drones and robotic systems that cannot rely only on GPS or external tracking.

Why it matters

Optical flow provides an independent signal for stability and navigation. It is crucial when sensors are limited and environments are unpredictable.

What I contributed

Experimented with flow based logic on micro drones, built intuition for how noise, texture, and speed affect readings, and linked that understanding back into control routines.

Dialogue level AI safety pipeline

What the system does

Watches entire conversations instead of isolated prompts, models attack patterns across turns, and routes interactions through layered checks that understand context.

Why it matters

Real misuse and jailbreak attempts evolve over multiple messages. A system that treats prompts in isolation will always be behind.

What I contributed

Defined an interaction centric risk view, drafted a pipeline where input, processing, and output layers are all guarded, and proposed metrics that show how effective each layer is.

Layered cyber defense models

What the system does

Uses metrics like PISR, ILVI, DCR, and UTCE to quantify how vulnerable or resilient an AI system is under adversarial pressure and user over reliance.

Why it matters

Teams need more than gut feeling about safety. Metrics create a shared language for engineers, researchers, and decision makers.

What I contributed

Co designed the metric set, aligned it with real attack families, and mapped it into a layered defense pipeline that can be implemented and measured in practice.
