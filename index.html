From raw curiosity to engineered intelligence

I did not wait for perfect timing or perfect resources. I built my lane by taking on systems that looked complex from the outside and making them predictable from the inside. Every constraint became fuel. Every failure became data. Every challenge turned into a sharper execution loop.

My path across autonomous robotics, AI security, adversarial machine learning, and systems engineering is driven by a simple rule. If a system exists, its risks can be mapped. If its risks can be mapped, it can be secured. Once it is secured, it can scale with confidence.

Today I focus on engineering trustworthy AI, strengthening dialogue-level defenses for LLMs, and designing semi-autonomous robotic platforms that stay stable and useful in the real world, not only in controlled environments.
