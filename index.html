Mental models

How I think about systems.

These aren’t slogans. They’re the filters I use when deciding what to build, what to say no to, and how to judge whether something is actually safe.

Interaction chains over components

Systems don’t fail in a vacuum. They fail at the joints where humans, agents, tools, and policies interact. I design around those joints.

Safety before scale

Scaling a fragile system just multiplies risk. I care more about whether a small loop is robust than whether a big system looks impressive.

Nature as a design review

Ecosystems have already solved stability, redundancy, and adaptation at scale. If an architecture contradicts those principles, I assume it will break.

Architecture is a promise

The structure of a system is a long-term promise about how it will behave. I treat architecture as seriously as the current performance numbers.
